Are you eligible for a traineeship?

A traineeship is a course designed to prepare someone for an apprenticeship or get them ready for work. They can take up to 6 months.

You can apply for a traineeship if you’re all of the following:

eligible to work in England

unemployed and have little or no work experience

What does this mean?

You can only apply for a traineeship if you’re unemployed (or are working less than 16 hours a week)

aged 16 to 18 and qualified below Level 3, or aged 19 to 24 and qualified below Level 2

More about qualifications

Level 2 qualifications are equivalent to 5 GCSEs (Grades A*-C).

Level 3 qualifications are equivalent to 2 or more A-levels.

Traineeships are unpaid, but employers can sometimes pay expenses for things like travel and meals.

If you're unsure if a traineeship is right for you, contact the National Careers Service for advice.
